My journey into the world of Sonic the Hedgehog.

![image](/assets/images/sega-and-sonic-the-hedgehog/sega-bell.jpeg)

<!--more-->

It started in a hotel in Turkey, where I noticed a Sega All-Stars Racing arcade machine featuring Sonic the Hedgehog. I didn’t play it, but it looked interesting — I used to love Sonic on my Game Gear.

A month later, while walking along the seafront in Great Yarmouth, I headed into [Williamson's Victorian Penny Arcade](https://oldpennyarcade-norfolk.com). A member of staff pointed out a fruit machine: a 1950s Sega Bell made by the same Sega that produced the Sonic games I played as a child.

Back home, while reading up on this fruit machine, I realised I lived only a short distance from Sega’s European office in Brentford. I had to see it and the giant Sonic the Hedgehog mural on the side for myself. (Sadly that is gone now since they moved offices to Chiswick)

![image](/assets/images/sega-and-sonic-the-hedgehog/sega-europe.jpeg)

The Sonic bug bit me. It led to me playing as many 2D Sonic platform games as I could find on iOS and Nintendo Switch.

But I wanted more. I regretted not playing Sega All-Stars in Turkey, and I started wondering what other Sonic arcade machines are in existence. Before long, I had a list of machines I need to try:

## Segasonic the Hedgehog
### Status: Played
![image](/assets/images/sega-and-sonic-the-hedgehog/segasonic-the-hedgehog.jpeg)

Released exclusively in Japan as an arcade-only title around the time of Sonic 2. It is a 3D game, and because it uses a trackball control it has never been re-released for home systems.

I assumed I’d never get to play this. A trip to [Arcade Odyssey](https://www.arcadeodyssey.com) in Florida or [Galloping Ghost Arcade](https://www.gallopingghostarcade.com) in Illinois would have been a hard sell to the family. It was still number one on my must-play list. Thankfully, as of June 2025, [The Heart Of Gaming](https://www.heartofgaming.co.uk) in Croydon has the only publicly available machine in the UK. I went there as soon as I could. The quirky 3D trackball gameplay is really fun. As it is on free play I managed to reach the final stage before the timer beat me.

## Rad Mobile
### Status: Played
![image](/assets/images/sega-and-sonic-the-hedgehog/rad-mobile.jpeg)

This is Sonic’s first appearance in a computer game; a cameo role as a hanging car ornament. It was released eight months before Sonic 1.

I read a social media post saying that [Pixel Bunker](https://www.thepixelbunker.com) in Milton Keynes doesn’t have any Sonic games, so I was ecstatic to find this one there while visiting in May 2026 along with Brickz and the Sci-Fi Museum.

## Segasonic Popcorn Shop
### Status: Unplayed

How brilliant is this? A Sonic mini-game where you get real popcorn. The only confirmed location I can find is from this [YouTube video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NEkbnszXrQE) in Tokyo's German Village.

## Sonic the Fighters / Championship
### Status: Unplayed

Although available on consoles, this was originally an arcade game so remains on my list. So far I haven’t been able to locate one in the UK.

## New UFO Catcher
### Status: Unplayed
Not a Sonic game, but these machines use the same audio chip as the Mega Drive / Genesis and play a selection of songs from the first Sonic games. [ClawMachineWiki](https://clawmachinewiki.fandom.com/wiki/New_UFO_Catcher) lists a few locations, but none in the UK.

## Sonic All-Stars Racing
### Status: Unplayed

I’m kicking myself for not trying this out when I first saw it in Turkey, so it’s now firmly on the list. This page says there is one in [Bournemouth and Rotherham](https://sonic.fandom.com/wiki/Sega_Amusements), and [Zenius I Vanisher](https://zenius-i-vanisher.com) suggests a few more locations, though I’m not sure how up to date that is.

I visited Bournemouth in 2026 and the machine wasn't there, although I was happy to find this Sonic ride-on from the All-Stars era.
