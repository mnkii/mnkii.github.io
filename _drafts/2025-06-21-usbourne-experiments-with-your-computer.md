As a child there was a computer book I would repeatedly borrow from my local library, and spend hours typing the code into my Commodore 64. I vividly remember the programs and the cute robot pictures, but the name escaped me.

30-odd years later I found the book online; "Experiments With Your Computer". Usbourne have generously made this and
other titles in the series [available for free online](https://usborne.com/gb/books/computer-and-coding-books), but
after holding my nerve long enough, I got a physical copy at not a completely shocking price.

There was one section, "Experiments With Sensors" which really captured my imagination; you get a blank plug which fits into the joystick port, solder on a resistor along with either a heat sensor or light sensor, then carry out important experiments such as plotting a graph of hot water cooling down, or pointing the light sensor out of your window to capture a burgalar sneaking past your property. This is Raspberry Pi 27 years before Raspberry Pi.

Being the early 90's I was hit with a problem; AliExpress or eBay were yet to exist... I managed to get the joystick socket from Tandy, but I had nowhere to get the other components from to complete the project. Burgalars have assumably been
walking past my house undetected ever since. It is time to put a stop to this.

First up is the "Coin Toss" program on the VICE C64 emulator.
